Purpose

This project aims to adapt and co-design pulmonary rehabilitation (PR) tools for people with Alpha-1 Antitrypsin Deficiency (AATD). It focuses on making PR more personalised, practical, and follow-up friendly by embedding a Treatable Traits (TT) framework and applying Experience-Based Co-Design (EBCD) with patients and clinicians.

The goal is to build tools that help both patients and healthcare professionals integrate personalised PR planning into daily care routines and long-term self-management.

Why AATD-tailored PR?

  • People with AATD often face unique challenges (e.g., fluctuating breathlessness, anxiety, oxygen setup, deconditioning).
  • Generic PR models may miss AATD-specific needs and the importance of structured follow-up.
  • Patients value clear, portable tools—targets, pacing, “good/bad day” cues, and flare plans—that help sustain activity after PR.

What this hub includes

  • Trait→Plan checklist (A4): maps traits to actions for clinicians.
  • Exercise Passport (A6): patient booklet with targets, RPE guidance, and simple progression rules.
  • Stay-Active Kit: quick-use cards (ramp plan, pacing, breathing, habit tracker, relapse/flare plan).
  • Education for Healthcare Professionals: short learning materials on AATD-specific PR adaptations, referral, and follow-up.
  • Workshop materials: agendas, case study, and usability tasks.
  • Outputs: visual abstract, reports, and educational slides as the project develops.

Who’s involved

This co-design process involves people with AATD, caregivers, PR clinicians, and researchers at the University of Birmingham. A parallel aim is to support education and awareness among healthcare professionals who deliver or refer to PR, ensuring that AATD traits are recognised and addressed in practice.

The project builds on a multi-method research programme:

  • Systematic review — Chronic Obstr Pulm Dis (2024)
  • Cohort analysis — Thorax (2025)
  • Qualitative interviews — ERS Open Research (2025)
  • Delphi consensus — 3 rounds (2024–25)
